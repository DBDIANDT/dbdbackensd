INTERPRETATION POLICY STANDARDS AND REGULATIONS

By accepting this position, you agree to abide by the following professional standards:

Interpreter Code of Ethics

Confidentiality: All information must be regarded as confidential.
Accuracy: The content and essence of the communication should be accurately represented.
Completeness: All spoken or written statements must be conveyed in their entirety.
Non-judgmental Attitude: Maintain neutrality regarding the content to be interpreted or translated.
Attitude Toward Clients: Consistently endeavor to cultivate a relationship founded on trust and respect with the client.
Acceptance of Assignments: It is advisable to decline or withdraw from assignments if one's level of competency or personal feelings hinder compliance with any of the conditions.
Professionalism: Interpreters are expected to arrive punctually, be adequately prepared, and dress in an appropriate manner.

Best Practices for Interpreters

  • Arrive on time and maintain a professional demeanor
  • Dress conservatively; a suit is mandatory for legal proceedings
  • Introduce yourself as an interpreter and inform both parties of your ethical obligations
  • Completely and accurately render spoken words without altering, omitting, or adding your own words
  • Present your qualifications accurately when requested
  • Speak loudly and clearly, using simple and concise terms
  • Exude confidence in your role as the expert interpreter
  • Adopt the first-person perspective in communication
  • Maintain impartiality and objectivity at all times
  • Disclose any potential conflicts of interest
  • Avoid engaging in conversations outside the scope of your responsibilities
  • Safeguard the confidentiality of all privileged information
  • Communicate any reservations about your capacity to fulfill an assignment
  • Politely decline any gifts or compensation exceeding authorized remuneration

Required Credentials and Skills

  • Language Proficiency: Exemplary command of both target language and English
  • Practice: Continuous practice using various materials and settings
  • Attention Skills: High level of concentration for complete analysis
  • Analytical Skills: Ability to construe meaning under time constraints
  • Memory Skills: Retention of messages for accurate rendering
  • Language Transfer Skills: Rapid and efficient expression finding
  • Note-taking Skills: Systematic approach specific to interpretation tasks
  • Cultural Awareness: Understanding of cultural references and concepts
  • Clear Speech: Audible and understandable communication
  • Appropriate Demeanor: Professional conduct suitable to setting
  • Adaptability: Stamina and stress management capabilities
  • Subject Matter Knowledge: Understanding of relevant terminology

Dress Code Guidelines

  • Professional business attire required
  • Clean, well-groomed appearance
  • Clothing free of holes, tears, or excessive wear
  • No offensive or inappropriate designs
  • Conservative, non-revealing clothing
  • Religious or ethnic dress styles are exempt from standard guidelines

Reporting Requirements

Contact our Quality Control team IMMEDIATELY to report:
  • Patient lateness or no-shows
  • Missing patient records at appointment location
  • Location changes from original confirmation
  • Additional services requested
  • Interpreter delays for any reason
  • Any concerns related to assignments
Important: It is prohibited to provide transportation services due to liability purposes.
Email follow-up appointments to our scheduling department with complete details in a timely manner.
